2025 Cropping Season Field G7  |  Research question: Is corn yield different in areas with legume sod plowed under before first cutting vs. after cutting once?
⬇ Download PDF

Welcome to your 2025 farm report. This report summarizes the on-farm nitrogen experiment conducted in Field G7, where plots with legume sod plowed under before first cutting (Treatment) were compared against plots plowed under after cutting once (Control).

Take-home message: Across the four nitrogen indicators — NBI, Chlorophyll Index, Biomass %N, and CSNT — treatment plots showed mixed responses compared to the control. The treatment increased chlorophyll relative to the control, indicating improved early-season plant vigor, but this did not translate into consistent improvements in overall nitrogen status or end-of-season sufficiency. With both treatments showing adequate nitrogen levels and high soil quality, the marginal benefit of the treatment appears limited under these conditions.

🌿 Nitrogen Balance Index (NBI)

The Nitrogen Balance Index (NBI) is measured with the Dualex sensor and reflects the ratio of chlorophyll to flavonoids in the leaf. A higher NBI indicates better nitrogen status: the plant has enough N to build chlorophyll without accumulating excess flavonoids as a stress response. Two leaves per plant were measured and averaged. Results are compared between Control and Treatment strips using a two-sample t-test.

🍃 Chlorophyll (Chl)

The Chlorophyll Index (Chl) is also measured with the Dualex sensor and estimates the amount of chlorophyll in the leaf. Chlorophyll is directly linked to the plant’s capacity for photosynthesis and is a reliable proxy for nitrogen sufficiency. Higher values indicate greener, more nitrogen-sufficient leaves. As with NBI, two leaves per plant were averaged and treatments compared with a t-test.

🧪 C:N in Corn Biomass

Corn biomass was collected at the V6 stage and sent for laboratory C:N analysis. This measures the actual nitrogen concentration in the plant tissue, a direct indicator of how much N the plant was able to take up. Note: sample sizes are small (n = 5 Control, n = 4 Treatment), so results are descriptive. Effect sizes (Cohen’s d) are reported instead of p-values.

🌽 Cornstalk Nitrate Test (CSNT)

The Cornstalk Nitrate Test (CSNT) measures nitrate concentration in the lower cornstalk at the end of the season. It is a post-harvest diagnostic tool: values above 250 ppm suggest the crop had sufficient nitrogen; values below 250 ppm suggest nitrogen was limiting. Because only one sample per treatment was collected, no statistical comparison is possible — this is a field-level observation.

🌱 Soil Health Assessment

Soil health was assessed using the Cornell Soil Health Test. The overall quality score was 76/100 (High). Most indicators were in the High to Very High range. Key areas to monitor are Surface Hardness (score 53-Medium), Subsurface Hardness (score 51-Medium), and Active Carbon (score 52-Medium), all indicating some compaction and biological activity that could be improved.

📝 Conclusion

🌿 Leaf Nitrogen Status (NBI)
Treatment plots showed a 0.7% higher NBI compared to Control (59.9 vs 59.5). This difference was (p = 0.888), indicating no statistically significant difference in in-season nitrogen status between treatments.
🍃 Chlorophyll Index (Chl)
Chlorophyll values were higher in Treatment plots (+9.1%; p = 0.00239). This statistically significant difference is consistent with improved nitrogen uptake in Treatment plots.
🧪 Biomass Nitrogen (%N)
Laboratory analysis of corn biomass showed Treatment plants had -3.7% less nitrogen in their tissue (3.775% vs 3.92%). The effect size (Cohen's d = 1.24) suggests a meaningful biological difference, though the small sample size prevents formal statistical testing.
🌽 End-of-Season CSNT
The Cornstalk Nitrate Test showed Control at 661 ppm and Treatment at 771 ppm (threshold: 250 ppm). Both treatments had sufficient nitrogen at season end.
